Children should be safe, not at work.

We oppose the exploitation of child labor under the guise of education.

The MESEM system, where children are forced to work in heavy and dangerous jobs under the guise of education, has turned into a structural problem threatening children's right to life and right to education.

ABOUT US

We are an independent civil society initiative committed to defending the fundamental rights of working children in Türkiye—especially those enrolled in MESEMs (Vocational Training Centers). Our work focuses on protecting children’s right to life, safety, and education by raising public awareness, monitoring violations, advocating for policy change, and amplifying the voices of children whose rights are at risk within the vocational training system.

PROBLEM

Children working within the MESEM program are frequently exposed to unsafe, unhealthy, and exploitative working conditions. Many are forced to work long hours for extremely low wages while facing intense physical and psychological pressure from employers. These conditions significantly increase the risk of workplace accidents, injuries, and long-term health problems. At the same time, child labor is gradually being normalized—especially in the textile industry—where children are treated as cheap, replaceable labor rather than individuals with fundamental rights to safety, education, and a dignified childhood.

OUR AIM

To make child labor visible and to develop child rights–based policy recommendations for MESEM practices by documenting rights violations, raising public awareness, and advocating for regulations that prioritize children’s safety, education, well-being, and long-term development over economic interests.
